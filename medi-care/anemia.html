Defination

    It is a clinical condition characterised by reduced haemoglobin concentration according to the age and sex of the individual.

Classification

  • Etiological (based on cause)
  • Morphological(Based on morphology of RBC)
    Etiological
  • Acute-truma,postpartum bleeding,haematemesis,melaena
  • Chronic-hook worm,haemorrhoid,excessive menstrual loss
  • Iron Deficiency
    Morphological
  • Genetic-red cell membrane defect
  • Acquired-autoimmune,toxic,mechanical and infectious causes
  • Haemoglobin abnormality

Causes of Normocytic Normochromic Anaemia/h2>
  • Haemorrhagic
  • Haemolytic anemia
  • Aplastic anemia
  • Anemia of chronic disorder
  • Malignancy
  • Endocrine disease

Causes of Microcytic Hypochromic Anaemia

  • Iron deficiency anemia
  • Thalassaemia
  • Sideroblastic anemia
  • Anemia of chronic

Iron defiency anemia symptoms

  • Weakness,vertigo,dizziness,light headedness
  • Tingling and numbness
  • Anorexia,weight loss and palpitation
  • Breathlessness on exrtion
  • Eating unusual material, like dirt or paint

Signs

  • The patien is pale,anemic
  • Kolionchia
  • Glossistis,angular stomatitis

Investigations

  • Test to confirm iron deficiency anemia
  • CBC,with PBF
  • serum iron,TIBC and ferritin
  • Stool for ova or cyst of hookworm and occult blood test
  • Bone marrow-shows erythoid hyperplasia

Teartment

  • Severe anemia should be corrected by blood transfusion
  • Iron therapy-oral ferrous sulphate,ferrous gluconate
