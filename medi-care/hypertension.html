    Defination: Persistent rise of arterial BP above the arbitrarily normal range. If systolic blood pressure > 140 mm Hg, diastolic BP > 90 mm Hg, the patient is diagnosed as hypertensive./ul>

    Causes

    • Primary or essential hypertension (95%)—cause unknown.
    • Secondary (5%)—/li>
    • Chronic glomerulonephritis./li>
    • Renal artery stenosis
    • Cushing syndrome
    • Acromegaly
    • Hyperthyroidism
    • Alcohol
    • Steroid

    Clinical features (symptoms)

    • May be asymptomatic, detected during routine examination.
    • Headache, dizziness, giddiness, insomnia, blurring of vision.
    • Features of complication—heart failure, CVD, renal failure etc.

    First degree AV block causes

    • Normally found in athletes(due to increased vagal tone)
    • Drugs
    • Acute myocardial infraction
    • Acute rheumatic carditis
    • In elderly (atherosclerosis)
    • Hyperkalaemia

    Complications of Hypertension

    • Acute left ventricular failure.
    • Drugs
    • Dissecting aneurysm.
    • Renal failure.
    • CVD (intracerebral haemorrhage)

    Treatment for Hypertension

    • Diuretic—thiazide (bendroflumethiazide
    • ACE inhibitor- enalapril, lisinopril
    • ARB—losartan, valsartan, irbesartan
    • Calcium channel blocker—amlodipine
    • Others—Methyldopa (used in pregnancy

    Prevention of stroke

    • Avoid excessive cardio excercise
    • Avoid junk food
