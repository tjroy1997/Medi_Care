    Defination: acute bacterial meningitis is rapidly developing inflammation of the layers of tissue that cover the brain and spinal cord (meninges) and of the fluid-filled space between the meninges (subarachnoid space) when it is caused by bacteria

Symptoms

  • High fever with chill and rigor
  • Headache,nausea,vomiting,photophobia,drowsiness
  • Impaired consciousness
  • Convulsion
  • Pneumococcal meningitis may be associated with pnumonia,common in elderly and alcoholics

Signs

  • Neck rigidity
  • Positive kernig's sign (extension of knee with hip joint flexed causes pain pain due to spasm of hamstring muscles )
  • Positive Brudzinski's sign (flexion of the nect causes flexion of hip and knee)
  • Petechial or nonspecific blotchy red rashes in skin-in meningococcal meningitis with septicaemia

Treatment

  • Hospitalisation
  • Adequate nutrition and hydration
