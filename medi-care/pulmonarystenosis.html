Defination

    Pulmonary stenosis is a condition caused by a narrowing of the pulmonary valve opening. .

Causes

  • Congenital (most common)..
  • Associated with Carcinoid syndrome, Noonan’s syndrome, Fallot’s tetralogy. —
  • May occur, if there is rubella in pregnancy

Clinical features (symptoms)

  • May be asymptomatic.
  • Symptoms, such as fatigue, weakness and effort syncope may occur

Signs

  • Left parasternal lift and epigastric pulsation (due to RVH).
  • Systolic thrill—Present in pulmonary area
  • Ejection systolic murmur (ESM) in pulmonary area, which radiates to the neck (more on inspiration)/li>

investigation

  • ECG—shows RVH and RAH..
  • Chest X-ray—shows enlarged pulmonary conus, oligaemic lung fields, poststenotic dilatation of pulmonary artery
  • Echocardiogram.

Treatment for Hypertension

  • In mild case, compatible with normal life (no specific treatment).
  • IIn severe symptomatic case, when the pressure gradient is >50 mm Hg, balloon valvuloplasty is the treatment of choice..
